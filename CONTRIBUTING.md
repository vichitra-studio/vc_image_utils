# Contributing

Thanks for your interest in contributing to this project.

## The short version

- You **keep full ownership** of everything you contribute. We never ask you to
  assign or give up your copyright.
- You can still use your own contribution **anywhere else** — relicense it, sell
  it, put it in another project. Nothing here restricts you.
- In return, you grant the project a **non-exclusive** license to use your
  contribution, including the right to include it in both the open-source
  (AGPL-3.0) releases and any future commercially-licensed releases.

This is what makes the project's dual-licensing model possible while leaving you
in full control of your own work. The full terms are in [`CLA.md`](CLA.md).

## How to contribute

1. Open an issue first for anything non-trivial, so we can agree on the approach
   before you spend time on it.
2. Fork, branch, and open a pull request against `main`.
3. Keep changes focused; one logical change per PR.
4. Make sure the build and tests pass (see [BUILDING.md](BUILDING.md) for
   prerequisites and build commands).

## Signing the CLA

Before your **first** pull request can be merged, you'll be asked to agree to the
Contributor License Agreement in [`CLA.md`](CLA.md). It's a one-time step.

While the project is small, signing is simple: state in your pull request that
you have read and agree to `CLA.md`, e.g.

> I have read and agree to the terms of CLA.md.

(If the project grows, this will move to an automated check such as
[CLA Assistant](https://cla-assistant.io/).)

You only ever sign once; it covers all your future contributions.
